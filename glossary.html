Financial Glossary

Assets

Resources owned by an individual or company that have economic value and can be converted into cash.


Arbitrage

The practice of taking advantage of price differences in different markets for the same asset to make a profit.


Annual Report

A comprehensive report detailing a company’s activities and financial performance throughout the preceding year.


Balance Sheet

A financial statement that provides a snapshot of a company's financial position at a specific point in time, detailing assets, liabilities, and shareholders’ equity.


Bonds

Debt securities issued by entities such as governments or corporations to raise capital, where the issuer promises to repay the bondholder the principal amount along with interest.


Bull Market

A market condition characterized by rising asset prices and investor optimism.


Capital Gains

The profit earned from the sale of an asset like stocks or real estate.


Commodities

Basic goods used in commerce that are interchangeable with other goods of the same type, such as gold, oil, or wheat.


Corporate Governance

The system of rules, practices, and processes by which a company is directed and controlled.


Debt-to-Equity Ratio

A financial ratio indicating the relative proportion of shareholders' equity and debt used to finance a company's assets.


Derivatives

Financial contracts whose value is derived from an underlying asset, such as stocks, bonds, or commodities.


Dividend

A portion of a company's earnings distributed to its shareholders.


Earnings Per Share (EPS)

A company’s profit divided by the number of its outstanding shares, used to measure a company’s profitability.


Equity

The value of shares issued by a company or the value remaining in assets after deducting liabilities.


Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF)

A type of investment fund and exchange-traded product that holds assets like stocks, commodities, or bonds and is traded on stock exchanges.


Fiscal Year

A one-year period that companies and governments use for financial reporting and budgeting.


Fixed Income

A type of investment where the investor receives regular, fixed interest payments, such as bonds.


Forex (Foreign Exchange)

The marketplace for buying and selling currencies.


Gross Domestic Product (GDP)

The total monetary value of all goods and services produced within a country’s borders in a specific time period.


Hedge Fund

An investment fund that uses a range of strategies, including leveraging or trading of non-traditional assets, to earn high returns for its investors.


Index Fund

A type of mutual fund or ETF designed to replicate the performance of a specific market index, such as the S&P 500.


Inflation

The rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising, eroding purchasing power over time.


Initial Public Offering (IPO)

The process by which a private company offers shares to the public for the first time to raise capital.


Interest Rate

The proportion of a loan charged as interest to the borrower, typically expressed as an annual percentage of the loan amount.


Leverage

The use of borrowed money to increase the potential return on investment.


Liquidity

The ease with which an asset can be converted into cash without affecting its market price.


Market Capitalization

The total value of a company’s outstanding shares of stock, calculated as share price times the number of shares.


Mutual Fund

An investment vehicle that pools money from many investors to purchase a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities.


Net Asset Value (NAV)

The value per share of a mutual fund or ETF, calculated by dividing the total value of the fund's assets by the number of shares outstanding.


Options

Financial derivatives that give buyers the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an underlying asset at a set price before a certain date.


Portfolio

A collection of financial investments like stocks, bonds, commodities, and cash held by an individual or institution.


Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E Ratio)

A valuation ratio of a company's current share price compared to its per-share earnings.


Risk Management

The process of identifying, assessing, and controlling threats to an organization's capital and earnings.


Shareholder

An individual or institution that legally owns one or more shares of stock in a public or private corporation.


Short Selling

The sale of a security that the seller does not own, with the hope of buying it back at a lower price.


Stock

A type of security that signifies ownership in a corporation and represents a claim on part of the company’s assets and earnings.


Volatility

A statistical measure of the dispersion of returns for a given security or market index, often used to gauge market risk.


Yield

The earnings generated and realized on an investment over a particular period, expressed as a percentage of the investment's cost or market value.
